Executive Summary

This feasibility study evaluates the viability of establishing a 400 SQM greenhouse in Al Kharj, Saudi Arabia, focused on producing ornamental plants for sale and providing maintenance services to businesses in Riyadh. The project envisions employing 2 staff members at SAR 2,000 monthly salary each, serving 30 clients with monthly maintenance services at SAR 1,000 per client, and generating additional revenue through plant sales.

Our comprehensive analysis indicates that the project is financially and operationally viable with a projected break-even period of 4.7 years and a 5-year ROI of 99.8%. The total initial investment required is SAR 1,129,800, with monthly operating expenses of approximately SAR 18,800 and projected monthly revenue of SAR 42,000.

The greenhouse will leverage advanced climate control and irrigation technologies to overcome the challenging desert climate of Al Kharj, producing high-quality ornamental plants suited to the Saudi Arabian market. The business model's dual focus on plant sales and maintenance services creates a stable revenue stream and positions the venture to capitalize on the growing demand for ornamental plants in urban settings, particularly in Riyadh's commercial sector.

While the project faces challenges related to extreme climate conditions, technical expertise requirements, and competition, our analysis suggests these can be effectively managed through proper planning, technology implementation, and staff training. The project aligns well with Saudi Vision 2030's emphasis on diversification and sustainable development, presenting a promising investment opportunity in the growing ornamental plant sector.
